### Event Overview

The event marked the first presentation of Ookla's new solution aimed at assessing and certifying the internet infrastructure of businesses in the hospitality sector. As Stephen Bye, President and CEO of Ookla, stated: "High-quality, reliable, and resilient connectivity is no longer a luxury; it has become an expectation for every guest and a core element of a competitive, high-end hospitality experience."

**Event Organizers:**
- Information and Communication Technologies Agency (ICTA)
- Azerbaijan Tourism Board (ATB)
- Azerbaijan Hotel Association (AHA)

**Attendance:** Over 100 senior representatives from tourism, telecommunications, and infrastructure sectors

### Key Topics Covered in My Presentation

#### 1. **The Starting Point (2020)**
- **Infrastructure challenges:** Only 9% fiber coverage (227K premises)
- **Legacy networks:** ADSL/DSL dominant with 150+ fragmented ISPs
- **Performance baseline:** 10.5 Mbps download, 11.1 Mbps upload
- **Digital divide:** 30 percentage point gap between urban (~76%) and rural (~46%) areas

#### 2. **Transformation Through "Online Azerbaijan" Initiative (2021-2025)**
- **Fiber coverage expansion:** 9% ‚Üí 100% (13√ó increase)
- **Speed improvement:** 10.5 Mbps ‚Üí 88.8 Mbps download (8√ó growth)
- **Subscriber growth:** 1.35M ‚Üí 2.30M fiber subscribers (+950K in 2.5 years)
- **Technology shift:** xDSL collapsed from 55% ‚Üí 3%, Fiber grew to 95%
- **Premium speeds:** 100+ Mbps subscriptions grew from 0.2% ‚Üí 65.6%

#### 3. **World Bank Recognition (B-READY 2025)**
- **#5 Globally** in Internet Sub-Indicator (Operational Effectiveness)
- **#1 Regional Leader** among peer economies (Regulatory Framework)
- **Score: 83.05/100** in Communal Services (#25 out of 101 economies)
- **Key recognition:** Internet as essential infrastructure (like electricity, water, transport)

#### 4. **Impact on Tourism & Hospitality**
- **Current speeds:** Baku & major cities: 73-85 Mbps; Tier-2 cities: 58+ Mbps
- **Suitable for all hospitality applications** including business travel (25+ Mbps required)
- **Quality of Service (QoS) standards** developed by ICTA (2024-25)
- **Competitive positioning:** Regional parity achieved, international standards supported
- **Strategic advantage:** Connectivity is now a tourism asset

#### 5. **Geographic Transformation**
- **Nationwide coverage:** Transformation across all regions
- **Rural-urban divide bridged:** From 30pp gap to near-parity
- **Visualization:** Heat maps showing acceleration from 2022 onwards

#### 6. **Strategic Vision for Tourism (2025-2030)**
- Position connectivity as core tourism infrastructure
- Develop Azerbaijan as digital economy & hospitality hub
- Establish regional leadership in digital tourism
- **Call to action for stakeholders:**
  - Hotels: Market world-class connectivity as differentiator
  - Tourism operators: Design digital-first experiences
  - Government: Promote Azerbaijan as "connected destination"
  - Venues: Deploy smart tourism solutions

### About the Initiative

The Speedtest Certified‚Ñ¢ program leverages Speedtest's globally trusted testing methodology to deliver third-party verification of internet quality in hospitality venues. The certification process includes:
- On-site evaluation of radio frequency (RF) quality
- Real-world performance testing
